# Career Preferences

Strategic targeting guidance for resume variants, cover letters, and outbound responses. Read before each send. Update as goals shift — these preferences are not permanent.

## Role content

**Title-agnostic.** Staff, Senior, Principal, or any other label — the title doesn't decide. I've never been title-oriented. What matters is the work content, not the hierarchy position.

**Core work wanted**:
- High-level iOS engineering — architecture, mobile design, platform direction, hands-on senior-level IC work.
- Collaboration with adjacent teams — backend, design, product, QA — at integration boundaries.

**Out of scope by preference**:
- Deep implementation work on non-iOS platforms. Happy to collaborate at the interface level, but don't want to be weighted toward Android development, backend implementation, web work, etc. as primary role scope.

**Salary**: must be reasonable. No documented floor; evaluate per opportunity.

## Contract structure

**Preferred**: fixed-price contracts with a defined deliverable scope.

**Avoiding**: hourly staff-augmentation roles — time-and-materials with no scope commitment.

**Current contract engagements** (for reference):
- [[CLEAR - Senior iOS Subcontract 2026-present]] — subcontractor via Stride. Scope-defined architectural evaluation and mentorship.
- [[Jason Poremba - FirefighterSpot 2026-present]] — fixed-scope Upwork build.

## Resume framing

One iOS-focused summary variant (`Framing/Summary - iOS.md`) covers all targets. It deliberately avoids level-positioning at the opening — focus is on iOS experience and mobile design/architecture depth. The Principal Software Engineer title at Walmart is stated as a fact in the Experience block and referenced in the summary's closing sentence.

Level preferences surface in cover letters, outbound messages, and recruiter conversations, not in the summary framing. For principal targets, the Principal-at-Walmart fact carries the credential without the summary needing to lead with it.

Pillars (Agentic Tooling, iOS Architecture on Modern Stack, Cross-Industry Delivery) are level-agnostic and used across all targets.

## Notes

_Preferences may shift over time. When they do, update this file and both summary variants together, not piecemeal._
