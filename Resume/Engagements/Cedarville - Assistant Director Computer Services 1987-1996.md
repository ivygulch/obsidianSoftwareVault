# Cedarville - Assistant Director Computer Services 1987-1996

## Context

Nine-year promoted role at Cedarville College as Assistant Director of Computer Services. Responsibilities spanned architecture and delivery of campus administrative systems, Novell network operations for a 1200+ station campus, and technical leadership within the Computer Services department. Overlapping throughout with the Rotman & Sjoquist consulting partnership (1988-1996) and the Adjunct Instructor role (1982-2006).

## Role

Assistant Director of Computer Services — technical leadership over campus systems and applications, architecture of administrative and network tooling, supervision of technical staff, and hands-on development work.

## Achievements

- Architected and led development of a client/server system for secure database access (1995-1996) covering book rental and sales, dormitory checkout, and secure network requests for administrative data (transcripts, account information). #architecture #client-server #database #secure-access
- Developed DataServer — campus network system for students and faculty to request electronic copies of personal or work-related information via email (predated web-based forms). #email-automation #workflow #campus-systems
- Developed Novell tools and utilities for Cedarville's 1200+ station campus network to provide an integrated user experience during the growth of the World Wide Web. #novell #network #tooling
- Developed UniData query, import/export, and utility tools — released to the public domain and used widely by UniData RDBMS users. #unidata #open-source #database-tooling
- Designed and developed a distributed requisition entry and approval system (1992) with a rule-based approval workflow. #workflow #distributed #rule-engine

## Tech Stack

UniData RDBMS, Novell network environment and SDK, Delphi (client/server), SQL, early client/server and distributed systems technologies.
