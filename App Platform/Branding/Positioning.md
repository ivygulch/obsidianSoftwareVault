# Positioning

Positioning is the thing [[Voice]] and [[Visual Identity]] derive
from, not the other way around. Fill this out first. If it is vague,
so is everything downstream.

A positioning statement is useful when it is falsifiable: another
person could write a different one and you could point at evidence
that picks the right one. "Friendly yet professional" is not
positioning.

## The Five Answers

**For who, specifically?**
_(Not "indie devs" or "writers." A specific person with a specific
situation: "solo iOS developers who have shipped at least one app and
want to build a second while keeping a day job.")_

**What do they currently use instead?**
_(Name the real substitute. Often it is a spreadsheet, a notebook,
a Notion page, or nothing. The substitute is the true competitor.)_

**What is the switching trigger?**
_(What has to happen in their day for them to go looking for
something new? If you cannot describe the moment, you cannot reach
them.)_

**What are we, in one sentence they would say back to a friend?**
_(Not our sentence — theirs. "It is the thing that X.")_

**What are we not?**
_(At least three things we are explicitly not. Positioning is defined
as much by what you refuse as by what you offer.)_

## Differentiation (falsifiable)

One line, sharp enough that a competitor could not also claim it:

> _(e.g. "The only journaling app that refuses to support streaks,
> because streaks punish the week you actually need to journal." A
> competitor who supports streaks cannot steal that line.)_

## Proof Points

_(Why should anyone believe the differentiation is real? Evidence,
not adjectives.)_
