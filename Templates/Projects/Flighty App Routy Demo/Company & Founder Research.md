# Company & Founder Research

Research backing the [[Outreach Plan]]. Source links at bottom.

## The role — Sr. Full Stack Engineer
Job page: https://flighty.com/careers/engineer

**Responsibilities:** Own features end-to-end (concept → ship → iterate); build iOS UI with design; design/operate backend for real-time tracking, delay prediction, notifications; turn aviation-data complexity into reliable UX; make architectural trade-off decisions.

**Requirements:**
- 6+ years production iOS (Swift, SwiftUI, UIKit) **and** backend (Kotlin/JVM preferred).
- Proven end-to-end feature ownership spanning app + API.
- Strong SQL **and** NoSQL.
- "Deep curiosity about aviation and travel."
- Product intuition focused on UX quality.
- Self-directed, remote-first.
- **EU or US Eastern/Central time zone.** ← Doug is US Eastern ✅

**Tech stack (mirrors Routy):** Swift (90% SwiftUI / 10% UIKit), Composable Architecture; Kotlin, Postgres, Cassandra, NATS.

**Culture signals (verbatim):** team is "tiny, elite, product-obsessed," distributed across 7 countries. Wants people with "strong opinions about what 'good' looks like," who prefer "small teams with high standards over big orgs," who "can make and defend your decisions." "There is no 'other team' to throw it over the wall to."

## Founder — Ryan Jones
Former Apple employee; founded Flighty after a tweet looking for collaborators (~40 replies in the first hours; team came from that). Active on indie iOS dev forums.

**Product philosophy (load-bearing for the pitch):**
- Product-driven growth, not ads: "Every time we focused on product, we would've gotten basically more growth or free growth"; "Every time we chased marketing tactics, we stalled." Tried performance marketing 3x, abandoned it.
- North star: would the team want to use this themselves? Optimizes for usefulness, clarity, delight.
- Craft + shareability as the growth engine (Passport's "blacklight" effect; live flight-sharing links).
- Adopts new iOS capabilities day one (e.g., Dynamic Island).

**Implication:** he evaluates *craft*. A cluttered funnel or generic demo data is itself a negative craft signal.

## What Flighty already ships (don't duplicate)
**Passport** — personal travel map + lifetime/yearly stats, designed for social sharing:
- Past-flight map = **flat 2D heatmap**, line thickness by frequency.
- Year-in-review stats: flights, total time, airports, distance, most-flown routes, aircraft types (oldest/newest/top seat), delays.
- **"Share and compare with friends."** ← This is why the user-vs-user demo idea was cut.
- Free; imports from calendar; passport-style design for social.

**Routy's wedge (what Flighty cannot do today):** real 3D globe, *actual recorded ADS-B tracks* (not great circles), exaggerated altitude profiles, time-based animation/flythrough.
